Why We Act

Lack of skill diversity in founding teams is still one of the top reasons young startups fail - "Not the right team" was ranked #3 out of 20 reasons startups fail.

Source

Concurrently, when looking at the representation of minorites in tech, various outlets still report a staggering low minority representation within the technology sector. This gap is further evidenced by the higher failure rates of minority startups compared to non-minority companies, caused mainly due to absence of essential skills, insufficient business networks, and lack of access of access to capital.

The ACT Model

A well balanced team has been proven to increase success.

ACT House embodies the principles of the ACT Team Model (“ACT Model”) – an organizational framework that rests on the sole premise that every team needs an Architect (business mind), a Creative (designer), and a Techie (programmer). Together, these essential components are the cornerstone of a successful founding team.

>
  • Architect
  • Creative
  • Techie

  • The architect is proficient in understanding people and processes. This is the business minded archetype who embraces skills in user experience, systems architecture, user research, and market strategy.

  • The creative is the designer that makes sure the product is delivering the best customer experience — implementing best practices in marketing, brand identity development, UX design, and user interface design.

  • The techie is the person who builds the actual product and is the one who writes the code, resulting in technology that forms the building blocks of the business.
